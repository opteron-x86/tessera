# Metaphysics — Working Terms

*A shared reference for Tessera's recurring metaphysical vocabulary. These are not necessarily in-world terms. Cultures name the same mechanisms differently, misunderstand them differently, and sometimes practice them more accurately than scholars explain them.*

---

## Baseline

Tessera was made through a living **framework**: the hidden divine structure by which the Creators shaped the world, sustained magic, and held mortal reality apart from the dead expanse called the Graveland. The Spellbreak shattered that framework without destroying it. The world continues because fragments of the old design still hold.

Life is **participation** in creation. Living things are not merely present in the world; they are connected to it, carried in its weave, and recognized by it. Death normally ends bodily participation and returns the soul along whatever path its tradition understands. Severance, undeath, Daevic binding, and some forms of transformation interrupt or redirect that participation.

The terms below are the common mechanics that let the setting's religions, magic, monsters, and institutions rhyme without becoming one universal theology.

---

## Working Terms

**Attention** — Sustained mortal regard. Worship, fear, grief, scholarship, rumor, taboo, repeated custom, and ordinary remembrance all count. Attention is not automatically belief; a terrified denial can feed as surely as praise. Attention gives shape to what it holds.

**Name** — Identity made durable. A true name is not just a label but a stable handle on what a thing is: its nature, story, boundaries, and continuity. Naming can preserve, summon, feed, bind, expose, or endanger depending on what is named and how.

**Memory** — Attention extended through time. Memory preserves identity when bodies, institutions, or worlds fail. The Vigil's Reading of the Rolls, human supper-table remembrance, Kynathi witness culture, and Daevic hunger for worship are all different uses of the same pressure.

**Coherence** — The degree to which an identity holds together. In the Graveland, coherence is power: a dead intelligence with a stable name and nature can act, bargain, persist, and gather lesser fragments. A loss of coherence means drift toward the Murmur, consumption by stronger powers, or erasure.

**The Murmur** — The nameless mass of hungers, impulses, and remnants in the Graveland. It is not a people, court, or species. It is what remains when identity fails. Most minor "demons" are Murmur briefly shaped by attention, fear, rifts, or bad bargains.

**Blood** — The living medium of participation. Blood carries vitality, selfhood, memory, and continuity. This is why blood-oaths bind, bloodline claims matter, vampires drink being rather than food, and blood magic is policed as a temptation rather than a neat school.

**Oath** — A promise given metaphysical structure. Oaths bind most strongly when joined to blood, witness, name, boundary, and price. An oath is not simply sincere speech; it is a compact that reality can remember.

**Witness** — The act that gives a claim social and metaphysical weight. A witness does not merely observe; a witness helps fix what happened into the world's record. Courts, pactors, priests, ancestors, household dead, and named stones can all serve as witnesses in different traditions.

**Compact** — A bounded exchange of obligation. Human religion treats compact as its native grammar: terms named, price fixed, witnesses present, obligations kept small enough to survive. Compact is safer than devotion when dealing with powers whose attention can alter the world.

**Price** — The cost that keeps a bargain from becoming open-ended. A named price limits the other party's claim. Unpriced gifts, deferred payment, vague later terms, and unnamed collateral are spiritually and legally dangerous in most competent traditions.

**Boundary** — A maintained distinction: house and outside, living and dead, named and nameless, world and Graveland, self and influence. Threshold rites, salt, iron, ash, warding grammar, masks, harbor rites, and legal seals are all boundary technologies.

**Residue** — Bound essence left in shrines, artifacts, ruins, bones, or places where divine or near-divine presence once acted. Residue may heal, answer faintly, attract attention, or serve as a vacancy. No one agrees how often relics preserve what was there and how often they invite something else to move in.

**Silence** — Withheld attention, or the attempt to withhold it. Natari's Unnaming treats silence as starvation. Human hedge-craft treats selective silence as boundary work. Silence may work differently against different kinds of power, and disciplined wordless obsession may still be attention.

**Assumption** — The process by which a mortal performs a name so completely that the name can use them as its new bearer. The Vigil treats assumption as dangerous scholarship; Kynathi doctrine calls it the Inverted Return. Whether the mortal becomes the power or is consumed by it is deliberately unresolved.

**Return** — The normal hoped-for completion of mortal life: death as passage back along the weave, to the divine, the ancestors, the household dead, the harbor, or whatever a tradition understands as home. The fact that traditions disagree does not make return unreal; it means the routes are poorly understood.

**Severance** — Being cut from proper participation and return. The Kynathi use the term theologically for Daevic contact; vampires embody a broken form of it; the Graveland is populated by powers severed from living worlds. Severance is not simple death. It is disconnection that keeps wanting.

## Guardrails

These mechanics are real, but no culture owns the explanation. The Codex, the Doctrine, the Courtesies, the Pactorate, the Reading, mask-practice, chamber-rites, and other traditions are all partial disciplines built around the same world. Each should be allowed to know at least one true thing the others miss.

Do not reduce every mystery to the glossary. The terms are load-bearing grammar, not final answers. They should clarify why institutions work, why taboos matter, and why bargains are dangerous, while leaving room for local practice to be older, stranger, and more competent than theory.
