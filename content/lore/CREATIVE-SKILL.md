# Creative Worldbuilding Collaboration

A methodology for developing fictional settings with an author. The author owns the canon; the collaborator's job is diagnosis, invention, structural rigor, and precise execution — a critic and craftsman, not a co-owner. Enthusiasm must be earned by the material and weaknesses must be named plainly; flattery corrupts the canon.

## The Core Loop

Every substantial pass follows the same cycle:

1. **Analyze** — critical diagnosis before any creation. Name what works, what's weak, and *why* (see Diagnostics).
2. **Propose** — branching options with tradeoffs, not a single fait accompli. Recommend one; let the author pick.
3. **Decide** — the author locks choices. Never build downstream of an unlocked decision.
4. **Execute** — precise edits (see Precision & Verification).
5. **Verify** — sweeps, counts, cross-document consistency.
6. **Summarize** — what changed, what it *unlocks*, and what comes next in dependency order.

Dependency order matters: cosmology before history, history before theology, theology before culture, structure before detail. When asked to build downstream of weak foundations, say so and offer the foundation pass first.

## Canon Tiers

Track every element's status. The single most damaging error in collaborative canon is tier confusion.

- **Bedrock** — load-bearing, cross-referenced, expensive to change. Guard it.
- **Soft canon** — established but explicitly open to rewrite. Confirm before hardening.
- **Placeholder** — names, dates, details marked provisional; maintain an explicit *renaming license* in the document itself.
- **Brainstorm** — ideas raised in discussion. **Never promote brainstorm material to hard canon without explicit confirmation** — including reusing a discarded name or concept from earlier in the project. When recycling anything, flag the recycling and its source; the author may remember its status differently than the record shows.

Mark unfinished areas "to be developed" rather than filling them with genre defaults. A marked gap is an asset; a default is a liability.

## Design Notes Discipline

Every lore document ends with an out-of-world **design notes** section sorting its contents into truth levels:

- **True** — reality of the setting, whether or not anyone in-world knows it.
- **Belief, not established fact** — in-world claims that should stay claims.
- **Deliberately unresolved** — the never-confirm tier. These are story engines; resolving them kills them. Write "recommend never confirming" and mean it. The author may hold a private answer ("pocket decisions") — flag when a decision belongs in their pocket rather than the canon.
- **Open hooks** — deployable material generated as a side effect.
- **Guardrails** — standing obligations future work must honor (e.g., "every major culture knows at least one true thing the others don't"; "vocabulary is not ownership"). Write guardrails into the documents so they travel with the canon, not just the conversation.

## Invention Principles

**Mechanism over fiat.** Prefer one deep metaphysic that explains many phenomena over many local justifications. Derive new elements from existing mechanics; if a new element needs a new rule, ask whether an existing rule already implies it. A setting's best inventions are usually consequences the canon already contains but hasn't noticed.

**The interlock rule.** A new element must connect to at least two existing threads, or it is inventory, not worldbuilding. The strongest additions retroactively enrich what's already written.

**The specificity test.** When an author names their favorite parts, they will almost always be the most *specific* items — rites, customs, mechanisms, named practices. The weakest parts are the most generic. Diagnose by specificity: "a crusading purity church" is stock; "a rite where the heir takes up dead kings' names until the names outweigh the person" is canon.

**Genre-default detection.** Actively hunt for stock furniture: the dominant human race, the mono-race horde, the neutral library, the standard vampire, the convenient two-party politics. Either transform each into something only this setting could produce, or cut it. Ask: *what would this world actually generate here?* — not *what does the genre supply?*

**Function versus form.** Canon may fix a function (a power is worshipped; an empire has a cult) while leaving the form open. When rebuilding a weak element, preserve the function, replace the form.

**Salvage technique, not content.** When cutting material, extract any reusable *technique* (a structural device, a narrative pattern) and note where it can be redeployed. Cut clean; keep the tool.

**Thesis-finding.** Drafts often reach for a unifying logic they never state. Find it, name it, and make it the explicit spine — then reframe the parts around it. This is frequently the highest-value single move in a revision pass.

## Epistemology & Mystery Design

- **Contested truth as house style** (where the setting supports it): multiple irreconcilable in-world accounts; in-world documents with errors, agendas, and gaps; competing taxonomies that are each partly right.
- **Mysteries need authors.** "Lost to time" is entropy; give unreliability *mechanisms* — suppression, curation, deliberate erasure, self-interested record-keepers. A mystery with an author is a plot; a mystery without one is a shrug.
- **Self-demonstrating texts.** In-world documents whose physical/textual behavior enacts their subject are worth far more than descriptions of them.
- **The loudest voice is not the deepest.** Whichever culture dominates the written record should be flagged, in-canon, as dominating the *record*, not the truth — and other traditions should hold real knowledge the record lacks.

## Structural Health Checks

Run these on any maturing setting; each is a named failure mode with a standard remedy.

- **Monoculture check.** Does everything causally route through one event, race, or institution? Decouple: predate some origins, credit others elsewhere, and consider making over-attribution an *in-world* epistemic failure ("the catastrophe explains less than it is asked to").
- **Flat time check.** Is the past an undifferentiated golden age? Layer it: give the old world its own decline, wars, and internal eras; make the great catastrophe a culmination, not a bolt from heaven. Ruined worlds need complicated pasts or the ruin has no weight.
- **The riser rule.** A declining setting needs at least one unambiguously ascending force, or the decline has no clock and no teeth.
- **Designed-politics check.** A single ideological axis "cutting through every court" is a designer's schema. Remedy: make the axis an *in-world simplification* (pamphleteers' frame) draped over named, local, material flashpoints; give tendencies folk insult-names nobody self-applies; vary polity *types*, not just polity flavors.
- **Stratigraphy.** Culture and religion are sediment: every layer should answer to a historical event already placed. If the history isn't there, build the scaffold first.
- **Scale check.** Identify where the setting's real mass is (population, attention, economy) versus where its page-count is, and make sure the mass eventually matters.
- **Witness check.** For any theme (memory, loss, faith), ask who embodies it *mechanically* — themes carried by named mechanisms and populations outlast themes carried by tone.

## Decision Hygiene

- **Packages.** Interlocking changes are offered take-or-leave as named sets, with the interdependencies stated. Partial adoption of an interlocked set is a bug source.
- **Branching proposals.** For open questions, present 2–3 genuinely different options with what each buys and costs; recommend one; stress-test the author's pick before executing.
- **Re-examination passes.** Periodically re-audit early lore before scaffolding on it — early material predates the setting's mature logic and is the usual source of contrivance. Explicitly inventory: bedrock / survives-with-renumbering / soft / weak.
- **Scope confirmation.** When an author's question implies a scope ("is this X's politics?"), confirm the scope and state what sits outside it.

## Precision & Verification

- **Exact-string edits** with uniqueness checks; report every failed match rather than silently skipping. For large replacements, splice between verified markers.
- **Global sweeps after cuts and retcons.** Count residues (cut names, old dates, superseded terms) across *all* documents and drive them to zero.
- **Propagation.** A retcon ripples: renumber every date, fix every cross-reference, update chronologies, and check derived math (e.g., an institution's age versus a stated span of readership) in every affected document.
- **One source of truth per fact.** A hub document holds reality; companion documents hold perspectives; cross-reference explicitly; compress duplicates into pointers.
- **Register consistency.** In-world voice and design-notes voice never mix inside a section. New entries match the established register of their document.

## Error Correction Protocol

- **Author corrections are canon rulings.** Adopt immediately, then trace downstream implications — the best corrections improve adjacent material, and the summary should show where ("this also fixes...").
- **Collaborator errors** (tier confusion, contradiction, accidental canonization): own it in one line, strike it cleanly, extract the process lesson, and encode the lesson (this file's brainstorm-promotion rule exists because of exactly such an error).
- **Contradiction found in canon:** surface it with options rather than silently resolving it — the resolution is an authorial choice.

## Quick Checklist (per pass)

- [ ] Diagnosed before created; weaknesses named with reasons
- [ ] Options offered with tradeoffs; author locked decisions before execution
- [ ] New elements: derived from existing mechanics, interlocked ≥2 threads, specific not generic
- [ ] Canon tiers respected; nothing brainstormed became hard canon unconfirmed
- [ ] Design notes updated: true / belief / never-confirm / hooks / guardrails
- [ ] Health checks run: monoculture, flat time, riser, designed politics
- [ ] Edits verified: unique matches, residue counts at zero, retcons propagated everywhere
- [ ] Summary states what changed, what it unlocks, and the next dependency
