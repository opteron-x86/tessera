# Tessera - World Lore

## The World

Tessera is a declining post-Spellbreak world. Merata is the current center of civilization: human kingdoms, diminished ancient peoples, ruins, frontier regions, and places where reality remains damaged.

Current baseline:

- Magic still works, but less reliably than before.
- The Avara and other high gods no longer answer reliably.
- Pre-Spellbreak works remain unmatched.
- The world continues because enough of the old framework still holds.

---

## The Spellbreak

The Spellbreak shattered the hidden framework through which the Creators shaped and sustained Tessera. It severed the Creators' connection to the world, changed magic, weakened planar boundaries, and left some regions permanently damaged.

Major theories:

- **Kynathi Arrogance** - The Hegemony attempted a ritual beyond its capacity, possibly to command creation or elevate the Kynathi.
- **Human Expansion** - Mortal growth and extraction strained the framework. Human kingdoms often favor this theory.
- **Magic Exhaustion** - Centuries of large-scale magic gradually degraded the framework.
- **The Theft of Divinity** - Someone tried to take divine power from the Creators and failed. A Codex-influenced version treats this as an ascension attempt at the wrong scale.
- **Daevic Interference** - Graveland powers helped cause the break or made it worse.
- **The Appointed End** - Creation was always meant to end, and the Spellbreak began Tessera's scheduled dissolution. This descends from the Anchorite heresy and is rarely pursued openly.

No theory is confirmed. The record is damaged, politicized, and in some places deliberately altered.

## The Graveland

The **Graveland** is a dead astral expanse outside mortal reality. It is inhabited by powers that never became Creators, refused creation, or lost their own worlds. Mortals usually call them demons, false gods, devils, angels, saints, or returned gods. Elven scholarship calls them **Daeva**.

Before the Spellbreak, the framework made contact difficult. After the Spellbreak, contact became easier through rifts, dreams, broken leylines, possession, and reckless magic.

The Graveland also produces creatures. Scholars call entities native to the Graveland **Eldritch**. They distinguish these from **Aberrations**, which are Tessera-native creatures changed by Spellbreak energies or similar forces.

### Avara and Daeva

Old elven language preserves two divine categories:

- **Avara** - "those who joined"; powers bound to the making and sustaining of Tessera.
- **Daeva** - "those who did not"; powers outside that participation.

### Names, Attention, and Coherence

Graveland power depends on identity. A stable name, nature, and story create **coherence**. Coherent powers can persist, bargain, act, and gather lesser fragments.

Sustained mortal attention strengthens coherence. Worship, fear, scholarship, rumor, taboo, grief, repeated custom, and denial can all feed what they hold in mind.

Mortal charts use terms such as thrones, courts, orbits, Vigils, and principles. These are useful models, not reliable reality.

### Daeva Contact

Common forms of contact:

- dreams and visions
- possession
- voices or compulsions
- cults and bargains
- influence around rifts and damaged leylines

Daeva rarely manifest physically. When they do, the event should be exceptional.

### Mantling

The metaphysics allows for extraordinary cases for mortals to assume a Daevic name by performing its deeds, nature, cult, and story until the name has more reality in the mortal than in the Graveland. Death or ritual crossing completes the accretion.
