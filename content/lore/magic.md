# Tessera — Magic

*A companion document to the world reference. This file holds framework magic, post-Spellbreak instability, cultural magical approaches, and blood as the living medium of participation formerly summarized in `world.md`.*

## Magic

Before the Spellbreak, magic was a structured discipline — part of the underlying framework through which the Creators shaped and sustained Tessera. Practitioners worked within a reliable system, and the Kynathi in particular had developed it to extraordinary sophistication.

After the Spellbreak, the framework cracked. Magic still functions — the fragments of the old design still hold — but it is less predictable, less consistent, and occasionally wrong. Most magical practice in the current age is safe and functional. Wild magic events — spontaneous, uncontrolled magical discharge — are rare and tend to cluster near rifts, leyline fractures, or sites of significant Spellbreak damage. Places like Lethengarde, where the boundary between planes was deliberately and repeatedly probed, accumulate magical instability over time.

Different peoples have different relationships with post-Spellbreak magic. The Kynathi practice a refined tradition that attempts to reconstruct pre-Spellbreak methods, with uneven success. The Alnathi have developed approaches better suited to the current, messier reality, partly through their relationship with the Daeva. Human magical traditions vary enormously by region and culture. The Reshai practice of spirit veneration and ritual likely accesses something older and less dependent on the framework the Spellbreak damaged.

One thread runs beneath every people's practice: blood. In Tessera, blood is the living medium of the weave — the current through which a body carries its participation in creation. This is why blood oaths genuinely bind, why bloodline claims carry more than ceremony, why vampires are what they are, and why the practices mortals call blood magic are less a school than a temptation that every tradition polices differently.

## Magic Design Notes

*Out-of-world reference: what is true, what is belief, and what should stay unresolved.*

**True:** Magic was more structured and reliable before the Spellbreak. The framework cracked but did not fail entirely, so most current magic still works within surviving fragments of the old design. Wild magic clusters around rifts, leyline fractures, and damaged places. Blood carries participation, selfhood, memory, and continuity, which makes blood-oaths, bloodline claims, vampirism, and blood magic metaphysically consequential.

**Belief, not established reality:** Any culture's claim that its current magic is the truest continuation of pre-Spellbreak practice. Kynathi confidence that reconstruction can restore the old discipline. Alnathi confidence that Daevic-adapted methods are properly managed. Human assumptions that bloodline legitimacy is always cleanly readable.

**Deliberately unresolved:** The full pre-Spellbreak magical system. Whether magic exhaustion caused the Spellbreak, resulted from the Waning, or was a symptom of deeper withdrawal. How much of Reshai spirit practice, Myrathi Reading, Sehrati chamber-rites, and human hedge-craft depends on the damaged framework versus older or stranger mechanics.

**Open hooks:** A spell that works by pre-Spellbreak rules and therefore behaves dangerously in the present. A blood-oath case that reveals a lineage is carrying more than ancestry. A Kynathi restoration working that almost succeeds. A human hedge-practice outperforming formal magic. A riftborn method that treats broken magic as native rather than damaged.

**Guardrails:** Magic should remain institutional and cultural, not merely technical. Every magical tradition should have costs, taboos, failure modes, and social enforcement. Blood magic is not a neat school; it is a temptation every tradition polices differently.
