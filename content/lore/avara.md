# The Avara — Kynathi Ancestor-Gods

*A companion document to the world reference. This file holds the Avaric pantheon, Kynathi restoration theology, and the unresolved meaning of divine silence formerly summarized in `world.md`.*

---

## The Avara

The Avara are the pantheon of ancestor-gods venerated by the Kynathi. They have names, spheres of influence, and defined personalities. Kynathi belief holds that the Avara were the divine first inhabitants of Tessera — that the Kynathi are their mortal-born descendants, and that death is a return to the divine realm rather than an ending. Whether the Avara are the canonical gods of the world, or simply the Kynathi cultural interpretation of a more universal divine truth, is unknown. Other cultures across Merata hold their own spiritual traditions, which may or may not reflect parallel interpretations of the same beings.

Before the Spellbreak, the Avara were present. Kynathi liturgy and the fragments preserved at Tindaire describe a living orthodoxy: gods who answered prayer, guided their peoples, on rare occasions walked among them — and actively opposed the far powers that pressed against the weave from outside. That presence did not end at the catastrophe; it faded before it. Through the Waning the Avara answered less and less, and their silence since the Spellbreak is, on one reading, not a wound at all — merely the completion of a withdrawal already long underway. Kynathi orthodoxy rejects the reading with a vigor that measures its fear of it. The Avara are largely silent since the Spellbreak. They cannot manifest physically, do not perform miracles, and prayer is an act of faith rather than reliable communication. On extremely rare occasions, an Avara may speak and act through a mortal host — typically someone of extraordinary devotion — usually at the cost of that person's life. Shrines, artifacts, and ruins retaining some bound essence of the divine are highly sought by priests, zealots, adventurers, and relic hunters. Some heretics seek to destroy them: from ideology, from Daevic influence, from iconoclasm, or from a desire to drain the residual power for themselves.

What the silence *is* remains the deepest question in Kynathi theology. Orthodoxy holds it to be a wound: the Spellbreak severed the connection, and restoration is possible — the hope around which Natari's entire civilization is organized. The old Anchorite reading, anathema at Natari, holds it to be a departure: the Avara are not silenced but withdrawing, as makers do when a world's course is run. Nothing in seven centuries has settled the question, and the two readings describe two different worlds — one wounded and waiting, one ending on schedule.

**Aevorn** — *The Radiant; Creation & Magic.* Chief of the Avaric pantheon. The god of the divine creative act, whose bloodline the Kynathi believe flows through them. Aevorn's silence since the Spellbreak is the deepest wound in Kynathi civilization — the central theological crisis around which Natari's edicts, their hostility to outsiders, and their obsession with restoration all orbit.

**Seranthi** — *Stars & Fate.* The cosmic order, prophecy, and astronomical knowledge. Seers and stargazers serve Seranthi. The great observatory at Lethengarde was almost certainly built under her patronage.

**Varaneth** — *War & Honor.* Not a god of violence but of the conduct of war — discipline, valor, the line between a soldier and a butcher. Kynathi martial tradition is practiced under Varaneth's name. His temples are spartan; his worship is physical.

**Calidris** — *Craft & Making.* The divine patron of architecture, art, and the great works of civilization. The ruins of pre-Spellbreak Kynathi construction are, by those who still venerate Calidris, considered his greatest monument. His new works are not.

**Althindra** — *Memory & Wisdom.* The keeper of what has been known and what should be known. Her worship extends beyond the Kynathi — scholars and historians across Merata honor her in some form, though not always by that name. The great library at Tindaire is sometimes described as her earthly mirror.

**Syluneth** — *Nature & Life.* The living world and its long rhythms. For a long-lived people, the slow cycles of nature have different weight than they do for humans. Syluneth is patient, cyclical, and indifferent to individual fate.

**Morindel** — *Return & Passage.* The guide for those passing from the mortal world back to the divine realm. Kynathi theology does not frame death as ending but as return — a soul's journey home. Morindel oversees the threshold. He is venerated quietly and without fear. The Kynathi's deepest dread is not death but spiritual severance: being cut off from the return entirely — unable to reach the divine after the Spellbreak broke the path. This is what Kynathi believe Daeva worship ultimately risks.

**Luneth** — *Moon & Hidden Mystery.* The things that cannot be examined directly — knowledge at the edge of perception, the unseen. Luneth's worshippers tend to be mystics and those who deal in hidden things. Her domain overlaps most with what the Daeva offer, which is why some Kynathi regard her uneasily. The Anchorite heresy arose among her mystics and Morindel's, and orthodoxy has never let her cult forget it.

**Aeredyn** — *Time & Legacy.* The long view, the slow turning of ages, the persistence of what matters. Monuments, traditions, the act of remembering: these are Aeredyn's domain. For a people watching their civilization decline across centuries, Aeredyn holds a particular, melancholy weight.

## Avara Design Notes

*Out-of-world reference: what is true, what is belief, and what should stay unresolved.*

**True:** The Avara were present and responsive before the Spellbreak, and their withdrawal began during the Waning. Since the Spellbreak, reliable Avaric manifestation has ended except for extremely rare, costly host-events and residual divine essence in places, relics, shrines, and artifacts. Their silence is the central wound of Kynathi civilization.

**Belief, not established reality:** Kynathi claims that the Avara are the sole canonical gods of Tessera. Kynathi divine descent from the Avara or Urath. Orthodoxy's claim that the Spellbreak merely wounded the connection and that restoration remains possible. Anchorite claims that the silence is withdrawal on schedule rather than injury.

**Deliberately unresolved (recommend never confirming):** Whether the Avara are wounded, withdrawn, dead, transformed, unreachable, or acting through mechanisms no living tradition recognizes. Whether Avaric host-events are true divine action, residue, memory, or something using an old channel. Whether relic-parishes rekindle old divinity or create vacancies for other powers to occupy.

**Open hooks:** A host-miracle that kills its vessel and destabilizes Natari doctrine. A relic that answers too clearly. A restoration faction willing to spend witnesses, bloodlines, or cities to reopen the path. A human relic-parish and a Kynathi temple claiming the same fragment. Luneth's mystics finding something orthodoxy cannot classify without breaking its own rules.

**Guardrails:** The Avara should not become a solved counterweight to the Daeva. Their silence is load-bearing. Their cults should gain specificity through rites, residues, ruins, host-costs, and institutional responses rather than through simple miracle lists.
