# Tessera — Locations

*A companion document to the world reference. This file holds the major regions, cities, ruins, rifts, and the Loneridge legend formerly summarized in `world.md`.*

---

## Locations

**Merata** — The large central continent and primary setting of Tessera. Merata bears the scars of the Spellbreak in visible and invisible ways: rifts, dead zones, places where magic behaves strangely, and ruins from what was clearly once something greater.

**Arlengh** — The northern continent, sealed since the Spellbreak behind permanent storm and ice. Before the catastrophe it was inhabited — by the human peoples whose descendants settled Isenfall generations before the crossing closed, and by the giants whose orphaned hulks still circle the world's oceans. What Arlengh is now, no one knows: whether its peoples warred and annihilated one another, whether the ice simply took them, or whether something survives behind the storms. No expedition has returned in living memory, and most never set out.

**Isenfall** — The northern tundra and taiga region of Merata. Its people are culturally distinct from southern humans, descended from Arlengh settlers who crossed generations before the Spellbreak — a span that has itself become myth, since the catastrophe sealed the crossing behind them. Indigenous tribes predating those settlers have largely dwindled.

**Teutania** — The northwestern highlands of Merata, between Isenfall and the main human heartland. A distinct highland culture — martial, insular, proud — that has maintained independence from the Merathine Empire more through terrain than politics.

**Pritani Jungle** — A vast jungle in southeastern Merata, home to the Reshai. Dense, ancient, and largely unmapped by outside peoples.

**Sahridi Desert** — A large desert believed to have been formed by the Spellbreak. The region was something else before — what the buried Vahari ruins suggest was a sophisticated civilization. The Sehrati are its current inhabitants.

**Cassidor** — The Merathine imperial capital, situated at the fertile delta of the Maren river.

**Tindaire** — Home to a college and library.

**Lorindale** — A prosperous human kingdom in the fertile heartland south of the Maren (see [Geopolitics](geopolitics.md)).

**Seradon** — A southern coastal kingdom on the warmer shores of Merata; the coast most exposed to Myrathi activity (see [Geopolitics](geopolitics.md)).

**Vrasar** — A northeastern human kingdom, rugged in terrain and culture; effectively where the Merathine Empire's reach ends, and now the frontier facing the Banner-Sworn (see [Geopolitics](geopolitics.md)).

**The Vezhan Steppe** — The eastern grasslands beyond Vrasar's marches, running past the edge of any imperial map. Home to horse clans united for the first time in memory under a single Khal as the Banner-Sworn (see [Geopolitics](geopolitics.md)).

**Juna** — A northern kingdom between the Dyrathi mountains and the tundra of Isenfall; warden of Illuvinel's approaches (see [Geopolitics](geopolitics.md)).

**Natari** — A mostly-intact pre-Spellbreak city on the southern coast, primarily inhabited by Kynathi. Enforces strict edicts to maintain ancient Kynathi tradition and high culture. Capital of the Natari Dominion and the living heart of what remains of Kynathi civilization.

**Lethengarde** — A deserted city in the northeast of Merata.

**The Shattered Isles** — The center of Myrathi civilization, an archipelago in the Orathian Sea.

**The Orathian Sea** — The body of water home to the Myrathi and, occasionally, to Sea Giants. Increasingly dangerous for other seafarers.

**Nerebyasca** — The Alnathi necropolis on the Blightlands' stable edge — and *necropolis* is meant precisely: a city whose citizens are dead. The living of Nerebyasca — **the Vigil**, its lay servitors, and the pilgrims and petitioners who come and go — are custodians and a minority; the population that matters is interred in the terraced vaults descending from the surface toward the boundary the dead must cross. In the oldest stratum of Alnathi doctrine the necropolis is a **harbor**: souls bound to the Graveland in life are tended here at the boundary, kept, the old teaching says, from dissolving with a world that may not last. The Vigil does not preach this. Its practice implies it daily.

The tending is not metaphor but labor. Because identity is the substance of persistence, the Vigil maintains its dead the way other cities maintain walls: the **Reading of the Rolls** runs day and night in rotating vigil, the names of the harbored dead spoken aloud in perpetual sequence — remembrance administered as life support, for a name that falls from the Rolls is a soul the harbor loses. The practice is the memory economy operated deliberately, in both directions — the same mechanism that feeds Daeva, turned to keeping the dead intact — and the Vigil's mastery of it is the reason the order, in the person of **Ashvenn the Twice-Grieved**, could produce the **Nerebine Codex**, whose uncopyable Vigil Original remains here, consulted only under rite. Whether the Vigil are the Codex's librarians, its operators, or its jailers depends on who is asked. Whether the harbored dead can be *reached* — spoken with, consulted — is a question outsiders ask constantly and the Vigil answers never; Alnathi funerary custom implies an answer, and implies it quietly.

Nerebyasca admits outsiders, which surprises those who finally see it. Petitioners come from every people: scholars seeking the Vigil's correspondence, the grieving seeking rites, and — steadily, discreetly — the dying, come to ask a berth in the harbor for souls their own traditions consign to silence. The Vigil refuses almost all of them. *Almost* is the word the desperate cross a continent for.

**The Blightlands** — A region of Merata's interior permanently damaged by the Spellbreak. Unstable magic, rifts, and Aberrations are more common here than anywhere else on the continent. Most peoples avoid the Blightlands entirely. The Blightlands were Alnathi country before they were the Blightlands — the continental interior settled by the Anchorite exiles millennia before the catastrophe — and the Alnathi homeland sits on the region's relatively stable edge. Their tolerance for its instability is partly long practice and partly constitutional: paid for, in the oldest accounting, at the Spellbreak itself.

**Gloamwood** — A strange forest bordering a Spellbreak-affected region of Merata. Its character is altered in ways that defy easy explanation: light behaves differently within it, the fauna are unusual, and travelers report disorientation and a persistent sense of being watched. Were-creatures are associated with Gloamwood, as are Aberrations that wander from the adjacent damaged zone. Alnathi tradition connects the forest's were-creatures to Syrath's touch upon Tessera — a lineage of completed transformations breeding true; the Nerebine Codex raises the question and pointedly declines to settle it. Gloamwood is not considered safe, and it is not mapped in any detail.

**Illuvinel** — A pre-Spellbreak ruin in the north of Merata, accessible from the regions around Isenfall and Juna. Once one of the most magnificent cities of the pre-Spellbreak world: elegant towers of marble and glass, sky bridges connecting elevated districts, grand tiered plazas, magical lifts, cascading waterfalls built into the architecture, and hanging gardens terraced across entire hillsides. All of it is collapsed and overgrown now. The towers have fallen, the magical lifts are inert, the waterfalls long dry. What remains is haunting in its beauty — shattered glass still catches the light, marble facades still bear carved reliefs, and occasionally a magical system reactivates without warning. Access to the ruin runs through Juna, whose crown licenses expeditions and taxes recoveries — a wardenship the Natari Dominion regards as the sale of desecration (see [Geopolitics](geopolitics.md)).

**Arhebel** — A pre-Spellbreak ruin in the south of Merata, primarily underground. Where Illuvinel is a collapsed surface city, Arhebel is a network of ancient vaults and catacombs extending deep below ground. The surface presence is minimal — worn stone entrances, a few collapsed structures — but the depths are extensive and largely unexplored. Pre-Spellbreak technology occasionally still functions within Arhebel, and artifacts regularly surface in the black markets of Lorindale and Cassidor. The lower levels have not been mapped. What Arhebel *was* is contested: the recovered artifacts suggest a treasury, the architecture suggests an archive, and the doors — which are built to seal from the outside — suggest a prison. The uncomfortable finding, reported independently by several expeditions and pursued by none, is that the masonry changes below a certain depth: the deeper levels are older than the upper ones, and past a certain point the stonework stops being Kynathi.

**Isrannaes** — A ruin carved directly into a mountainside — corridors and chambers cut from and reinforced within living rock, and built *late*: the stonework dates to the Waning, and it was made in a hurry, by a civilization that did not hurry. Abandoned since the Spellbreak. Its location makes it physically difficult to reach; the deep interior chambers are sealed, whether by the catastrophe or by design, and many have never been entered in the centuries since. What the Hegemony was building in its frightened final age — a refuge, an instrument, a vault, or something else — is unknown, and whether Isrannaes was meant to survive the Spellbreak or to prevent it is the kind of question Tindaire assigns to scholars it wants to lose.

**Loneridge** — A frontier settlement built around a contained Spellbreak rift; originally a mining town, later a ghost town, now the unlikeliest boomtown in Merata.
