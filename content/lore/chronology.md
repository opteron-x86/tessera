## A Brief Chronology

Dates are reckoned from the Spellbreak (PS). All are approximate; the record is thin, contested, and in places deliberately damaged.

**Deep past** — The age of the Kynathi Hegemony, spanning millennia. The Anchorite schism and the Alnathi exile into the interior. The old human kingdoms are conquered or coerced into clientage. The Giant War breaks the Giants' strength. The Vorathi flesh-shapers rise and are destroyed — generations before the catastrophe, by causes contested. The Shattering of the Isles, after which the sea begins to speak. Settlers cross from Arlengh to Isenfall.

**The last age — the Waning.** For a century or more before the breaking, the world runs down: the Avara answer less and less, magic costs more, births thin among the long-lived. The Hegemony's final age is magnificent and frightened. Isrannaes is carved in haste. Whatever desperate remedies were attempted at the end — and by whom — is the substance of every Spellbreak theory.

**0 PS — The Spellbreak.** The framework shatters. Arlengh seals behind storm and ice. The Blightlands and the Sahridi Desert are made. The Alnathi transformation begins. Aevorn and the Avara fall silent. The First Night: across Merata, the deathbeds of the hour rise — the greatest event in vampire history, though not, the eldest insist, its beginning.

**c. 0–100 PS — The Sundering Years.** The Hegemony collapses to the Natari Dominion. The client peoples scatter into independence. Riftborn children begin to be born near the wounds. Vaelthir the Unspeaking restores the Unnaming at Natari.

**c. 100 PS** — The first Merathine unites Cassidor and the river kingdoms; the Merathine Empire is founded.

**c. 200–250 PS** — Ashvenn the Twice-Grieved redacts the Nerebine Codex at Nerebyasca.

**c. 250–450 PS — The imperial peak.** The empire's greatest extent. Tindaire is founded under imperial patronage.

**c. 450 PS–present — The fading.** Imperial recession; the successor kingdoms rise into the slack. The Lethengarde Incident empties the observatory city. The Loneridge rift opens and is contained by the Rift-Breakers within living memory. The Vezhan clans unite under a single Khal.

**750 PS — The present.** The eldest Kynathi witnesses of the world before are dying. The Banner-Sworn gather in the east. The empire recedes; the Dominion endures; the rifts remain.

## Chronology Design Notes

*Out-of-world reference: what is true, what is belief, and what should stay unresolved.*

**True:** The Waning predates the Spellbreak. The Spellbreak occurred at 0 PS. The present reference point is 750 PS. The Merathine Empire was founded roughly a century after the Spellbreak, reached its peak during the middle post-Spellbreak centuries, and has been receding for roughly three centuries.

**Belief, not established reality:** Any exact dating in the deep past. Clean causal ordering for the Giant War, Vorathi extinction, Shattering of the Isles, Anchorite schism, and late-Hegemony projects. Institutional chronologies that make their own founders more central than the record supports.

**Deliberately unresolved:** The precise duration of the Waning. The full sequence of desperate Hegemony projects before 0 PS. Whether the Spellbreak was culmination, accident, attack, attempted repair, scheduled ending, or several of these at once. What happened in Arlengh after the crossing sealed.

**Open hooks:** A chronology pass that stratifies the deep past into named eras. A disputed date whose correction would shift a religious claim. A recovered pre-Waning source that shows decline began earlier than anyone admits. A Tindaire chronological reconstruction opposed by Natari, Nerebyasca, and Cassidor for different reasons.

**Guardrails:** The past should not flatten into a single golden age followed by a single fall. The Spellbreak was the greatest event in world history, not the only event. Approximate dates are useful; false precision is not.
