# Tessera - Canon Spine and Timeline

*Companion to [world.md](world.md). This file tracks the setting spine, broad chronology, and open canon decisions.*

---

## Purpose

This is a working timeline, not a full history. Its job is to establish sequence, pressure, and cause without resolving every mystery.

Use three confidence labels while drafting:

- **Fixed** - Already established or needed as setting load-bearing canon.
- **Contested** - True status is unknown in-world, or different traditions explain it differently.
- **Open** - Needs a creator decision before it becomes canon.

The Spellbreak is year zero for relative dating. Exact year counts should remain provisional until the age of the current era is set.

---

## Canon Spine

These are the current load-bearing assumptions of Tessera.

1. **Tessera is a declining world, not a dead one.** Civilization continues because enough of the old framework still holds.
2. **The Spellbreak is the central rupture.** It damaged the hidden framework through which the Creators shaped and sustained the world.
3. **The cause of the Spellbreak is unresolved.** Every major theory is politically, religiously, or culturally useful to someone.
4. **The Kynathi Hegemony ruled much of pre-Spellbreak Merata.** The Natari Dominion is its legal and cultural continuation, not a new successor state in its own eyes.
5. **Human polities inherited the age after the Spellbreak.** Humans were present before the catastrophe, but their current dominance belongs to the successor era.
6. **Magic still works, but the system behind it is cracked.** Most practice is safe and functional; failures cluster near rifts, damaged leylines, and Spellbreak scars.
7. **Blood is the living medium of participation.** Blood oaths, bloodlines, vampirism, and blood magic all matter because blood carries more than life.
8. **The Avara no longer answer reliably.** Their silence is not simple proof of absence, death, refusal, or distance.
9. **The Graveland is easier to reach than it should be.** Daeva, Eldritch entities, dreams, possession, bargains, and cults all became more dangerous after the Spellbreak.
10. **Names, attention, and story have metaphysical force.** Coherence allows Graveland powers to persist and act; mortal belief can strengthen what it holds in mind.
11. **The record is damaged.** Public history is fragmentary, biased, altered, and in some places deliberately falsified.

## Working Chronology

### 1. Creation and the Deep Mythic Past

**Fixed:** The world was shaped and sustained through a hidden framework associated with the Creators and the powers later called the Avara.

**Fixed:** Old elven language distinguishes the Avara, "those who joined," from the Daeva, "those who did not."

**Contested:** Traditions describe the Elathi as a people before the current races. Kynathi theology treats them as divine first inhabitants and claims the Kynathi descend from them most directly.

**Open:** Decide whether the Elathi were literal beings, a theological memory of the Creators, an elder civilization, or a deliberately collapsed category that different peoples use for different things.

### 2. First Peoples and Divergence

**Fixed:** The elder peoples are related by older lineages and traditions, though the exact genealogy is not public or settled.

**Fixed:** The Kynathi and Alnathi began as one people before their split.

**Fixed:** The Dyrathi are linked to the Urath lineage and related to the other elder peoples. Unlike the Kynathi and Giants, they are not in obvious decline in the current age.

**Fixed:** Giants were already declining before the Spellbreak.

**Fixed:** Humans existed before the Spellbreak. Some human polities were free kingdoms before conquest, while others became clients, tributaries, or auxiliaries of the Kynathi Hegemony.

**Fixed:** Arlengh was once home to humans and giants.

**Contested:** The Vahari existed before the Sahridi Desert buried their ruins. Their nature, relationship to the Kynathi Hegemony, and connection to the Sehrati remain unresolved.

**Open:** Decide whether the elder peoples share a literal created ancestry, a mythic genealogy, or several incompatible traditions that all preserve part of the truth.

### 3. The Kynathi Ascendancy

**Fixed:** The Kynathi Hegemony rose to rule much of Merata.

**Fixed:** Pre-Spellbreak Kynathi magic reached a sophistication unmatched in the current age.

**Fixed:** Many pre-Spellbreak works remain beyond present craft: ruins, observatories, cities, waterworks, lifts, vaults, and other structures that later peoples can occupy, study, or fear but rarely reproduce.

**Fixed:** Natari was a major Kynathi city and remains mostly intact after the Spellbreak.

**Contested:** Human traditions emphasize survival under domination, negotiated service, older independence, or pragmatic cooperation depending on the kingdom telling the story.

**Open:** Decide whether Natari was the old imperial capital, a sacred/legal capital, a regional seat that became the fallback center, or something stranger.

### 4. Schisms, Heresies, and Elder Wars

**Fixed:** The Alnathi split from the Kynathi first over the heresy and the Daeva.

**Fixed:** The Anchorite heresy is old enough that the Appointed End theory descends from it.

**Fixed:** The Giant War against the Kynathi Hegemony broke giant strength. Its cause and outcome survive only in fragments.

**Fixed:** Neresce is the Alnathi necropolis, and the Vigil maintains the Reading of the Rolls and guards the Vigil Original of the Nerescine Codex.

**Contested:** Kynathi, Alnathi, giant, and human accounts disagree about whether the Giant War was conquest, rebellion, punishment, containment, or betrayal.

**Open:** Decide whether the Alnathi heresy and the Anchorite heresy are the same event, related events, or later traditions that became tangled together.

**Open:** Decide whether the Nerescine Codex predates the Spellbreak, records its aftermath, or is itself a damaged artifact of the catastrophe.

### 5. Late Hegemony and the Pressure Before the Break

**Fixed:** The Hegemony stood over a world of client peoples, old free peoples, rival traditions, and elder powers in decline.

**Fixed:** The hidden framework could be strained, damaged, misused, or attacked, but no public theory about the Spellbreak is confirmed.

**Contested:** Major explanations include Kynathi arrogance, human expansion, magic exhaustion, theft of divinity, Daevic interference, and the Appointed End.

### 6. The Spellbreak

**Fixed:** The Spellbreak shattered the hidden framework through which the Creators shaped and sustained Tessera.

**Fixed:** It severed the Creators' connection to the world, changed magic, weakened planar boundaries, and left some regions permanently damaged.

**Fixed:** It made contact with the Graveland easier through rifts, dreams, broken leylines, possession, and reckless magic.

**Fixed:** Arlengh was sealed behind permanent storm and ice.

**Fixed:** The Sahridi Desert was created over buried Vahari ruins.

**Fixed:** The Blightlands became a permanently damaged interior region where rifts, unstable magic, and Aberrations are common.

**Fixed:** Sea Giants aboard the great hulks became cut off from Arlengh.

**Fixed:** The event damaged the record, creating room for propaganda, missing archives, altered histories, and sincere theological contradiction.

**Open:** Decide whether the Spellbreak was a single catastrophic moment, a short cascading disaster, or a named period of related failures.

### 7. The First Aftermath

**Fixed:** The Kynathi Hegemony contracted into the Natari Dominion.

**Fixed:** The Natari Dominion understands itself as the direct continuation of the Hegemony in law and identity.

**Fixed:** The Avara and other high gods no longer answered reliably.

**Fixed:** Magic became less predictable, less consistent, and occasionally wrong.

**Fixed:** Riftborn began appearing in the decades after the Spellbreak near rifts, leyline fractures, and blighted lands.

**Fixed:** Aberrations are Tessera-native creatures changed by Spellbreak energies or similar forces, distinct from Eldritch entities native to the Graveland.

**Contested:** The first generations after the Spellbreak are remembered differently by every surviving people: collapse, liberation, punishment, exile, emergency, or proof of prophecy.

**Open:** Decide how much organized continuity survived outside Natari, Neresce, the Dyrathi holds, and other protected places.

### 8. The Successor Centuries

**Fixed:** Human kingdoms and empires became central to Merata's current political order.

**Fixed:** The Merathine Empire is centered on Cassidor at the fertile delta of the Maren River.

**Fixed:** Lorindale occupies the fertile heartland south of the Maren.

**Fixed:** Seradon holds a southern coast exposed to the Orathian Sea and Myrathi activity.

**Fixed:** Vrasar occupies the rugged northeast where Merathine reach gives way to frontier pressure from the Vezhan Steppe.

**Fixed:** Juna sits between the Dyrathi mountains and Isenfall.

**Fixed:** Teutania remains effectively independent because its terrain is hard to conquer.

**Fixed:** Isenfall's settled peoples descend from Arlengh migrants who crossed before the Spellbreak sealed the north.

**Fixed:** Dyrathi holds survived the surface catastrophe better than most civilizations; some trade, while others remain closed and hostile.

**Fixed:** Myrathi civilization persists in the Shattered Isles, governed culturally by the Reading.

**Contested:** Human successor states disagree about whether they inherited, liberated, repaired, replaced, or avenged the old order.

**Open:** Decide whether the Merathine Empire is still expanding, has plateaued, is declining, or is trying to restore a lost high point.

### 9. The Current Age

**Fixed:** Merata is the current center of civilization.

**Fixed:** The Natari Dominion remains diminished, encircled, repeatedly written off, and still standing.

**Fixed:** Pre-Spellbreak ruins remain politically, religiously, economically, and magically important.

**Fixed:** Loneridge has become an unlikely boomtown around a contained Spellbreak rift.

**Fixed:** The Banner-Sworn have united Vezhan horse clans under a single Khal beyond Vrasar's marches.

**Fixed:** The Gloamwood remains an altered forest associated with strange light, broken navigation, were-creatures, and wandering Aberrations.

**Fixed:** Sea Giant hulk landfalls remain generational disasters.

**Fixed:** Vampires persist by drinking living blood, which carries vitality, memory, selfhood, and continuity.

**Contested:** Every major power treats the present decline differently: an imperial problem, a theological punishment, a frontier opportunity, a prophecy unfolding, or simply the cost of living after catastrophe.

**Open:** Decide the current date after the Spellbreak. A useful provisional range is several centuries after the event, close enough that the oldest Kynathi may preserve living memory if desired.

## Priority Canon Decisions

These decisions will clarify the next layer of worldbuilding.

1. **Current date:** Is the present within Kynathi living memory of the Spellbreak, or has even that passed into archive and myth?
3. **Spellbreak duration:** Was it a moment, a cascade, or an era?
4. **Natari's old role:** Was Natari the Hegemony's capital before the catastrophe or the place that survived well enough to claim continuity?
5. **Alnathi and Anchorites:** Are their heresies the same, related, or separate?
6. **Vahari identity:** Were the Vahari human, elder, mixed, non-mortal, or intentionally unknown?
7. **Merathine origin:** Did the Merathine Empire rise as rebellion, restoration, opportunistic consolidation, anti-Kynathi project, or client-state succession?
8. **Living witnesses:** Are there still Kynathi, Dyrathi, vampires, or other long-lived beings who personally remember the pre-Spellbreak world?
9. **Public truth:** Which facts are known to ordinary educated people, and which are restricted to temples, courts, ruins, or suspect scholarship?
10. **Authorial truth:** Which mysteries should have a private answer, and which should remain unresolved even for the setting bible?
