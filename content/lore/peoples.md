# Tessera — Peoples

*Companion to [world.md](world.md). This file tracks origin theories, major peoples, and post-Spellbreak changed peoples.*

---

## Origin Traditions

**Elathi** — Fragmentary traditions describe an ancient people who predated the current races. Kynathi theology claims the Elathi were divine first inhabitants of Tessera and that the Kynathi descend from them most directly.

## Elder Peoples

### Kynathi (High Elves)

The Kynathi ruled much of pre-Spellbreak Merata through the **Kynathi Hegemony**. The catastrophe reduced them to the **Natari Dominion** (see [Geopolitics](geopolitics.md)).

They are long-lived, slow to breed, and demographically unrecovered. A Kynathi may live nine centuries.

Their religion centers on the **Avara**, whom they treat as ancestor-gods.

### Alnathi (Dark Elves)

The Alnathi and Kynathi began as one people. They split first over the heresy and the Daeva.

### Myrathi (Sea Elves)

The Myrathi occupy the Shattered Isles. Their fast warships are feared across the Orathian Sea because outsiders cannot predict them.

Myrathi culture turns on **the Reading**: a tidecaller reads the sea, sky, weather, and encounter, then acts on that verdict. A ship may be ignored, aided, crippled, destroyed, or have one person taken. The Myrathi do not explain the pattern and may not understand it in outsider terms.

### Dyrathi (Dwarves)

The Dyrathi are linked to the Urath lineage and related to the other elder peoples. Unlike the Kynathi and Giants, they are not in obvious decline.

Their underground holds, mines, and cities survived the surface catastrophe better than most civilizations. Some holds trade with surface powers; others remain closed and hostile. Dyrathi craftspeople are renowned across Merata.

### Giants

Giants were already declining before the Spellbreak. The ancient **Giant War** against the Kynathi Hegemony broke their strength, though its cause and outcome survive only in fragments.

Most present-day Giants avoid other peoples except to defend territory or maintain old tribute relationships.

### Sea Giants

Sea Giants are Arlengh's orphans. When the Spellbreak sealed the northern continent, those aboard the great hulks were left without a reachable homeland.

They live entirely aboard three known **sea-hulks**, vessels large enough to be mistaken for islands. Hulk landfalls are generational disasters: cities lose timber, metal, food, livestock, and people, usually without message or negotiation.

Myrathi vessels are sometimes seen before a hulk arrives. This is omen or Reading, not a confirmed alliance.

## Human and Regional Peoples

### Humans

Humans are the most numerous people in Merata. Before the Spellbreak, many human polities were clients, tributaries, or auxiliaries of the Kynathi Hegemony, but older free kingdoms existed before conquest.

Human cultures range from Isenfall's Arlengh-descended northerners to Teutanian highlanders and southern trading cities.

### Reshai

The Reshai are a monkey-like people indigenous to the Pritani Jungle. Their animistic tradition venerates nature spirits and **Jakanu**, a trickster patron associated with cleverness, martial wit, and Reshai divine nature.

Masks carry ritual and social meaning. Their staff-fighting tradition is tied to Jakanu's legendary feats.

### Sehrati

The Sehrati inhabit the Sahridi Desert and rarely appear beyond its edges. They are broadly human in form, with dark hair, deep amber to dark brown skin, and frequent pale gold or amber eyes.

They wear elaborate desert attire patterned after sacred ruin-carvings. Ritual scarification is common. They treat the buried Vahari ruins as sacred ground.

## Changed Peoples and Conditions

### Riftborn

Riftborn began appearing in the decades after the Spellbreak near rifts, leyline fractures, and blighted lands. Traits include horns, tails, claws, night-sight, and unnatural skin or hair colors. The changes breed true.

They are thin-spread, mostly urban or frontier, and often scapegoated. The core question is unresolved: Aberration-touched, a new people, or an intentional product of the broken framework. "Riftborn" is the common-tongue label; larger communities use their own names.

### Vampires

Vampires are beings whose participation in creation is broken but not cleanly ended. They persist by drinking living blood, which carries vitality, memory, selfhood, and continuity.

A victim may survive diminished: memories thin, identity weakens, and heavy feeding can erase a person without killing the body. Dead blood has no current. Starved vampires do not die; they lose memory, name, and self until only appetite remains.
